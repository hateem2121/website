## Heritage Timeline

- **1889–1904:** Allah Ditta Ghafuree, a Sialkot leather artisan, began the business later formalized as Ghafuree Industries (1904) — pioneering alum chrome leather tanning, leather stretching frames, hydraulic stretching machines, and ball-lamination techniques.
- **1904–1980:** Allah Ditta Sandal led for 76 years, training 380,000+ workers and scaling Pakistan's daily football output to 200,000. His sons expanded the business: Muhammad Anwar founded Sandal Trading Corporation (1942, introducing Star of Pakistan hockey sticks), and Muhammad Sarwar founded Loyal Sports Pvt Ltd (1952, reaching European markets by 1958).
- **1956–1992:** M. Iqbal Sandal joined Loyal Sports in 1956. His innovations — PU-on-leather (1972), synthetic PVC/PU laminated footballs (1974), and fiber-texture lamination for perfectly round balls — helped bring Adidas to Pakistani manufacturing.
- **1992:** M. Iqbal Sandal and his sons consolidated the family businesses into Durus Industries (Pvt) Ltd. "Durus" means strength, durability, and endurance in Arabic.
- **2020–present:** Durus Industries, now an established sports-goods powerhouse, spun off RUN APPAREL as a dedicated B2B apparel division — separate from its sports-equipment business — to serve athletic brands, teams, corporate programs, and fitness organizations.

## Manufacturing Process

1. **Pre-Production & Planning** — client consultation, 3D virtual prototyping (CLO 3D, Optitex), certified material sourcing, sample approval. *Checkpoint: tech pack approved.*
2. **Material Preparation & Sourcing** — incoming inspection (colorfastness, shrinkage, tensile strength), fabric pre-treatment. *Checkpoint: materials approved for the floor.*
3. **Cutting & Initial Processing** — algorithmic pattern nesting (90–95% material utilization), laser cutting (±0.5mm tolerance). *Checkpoint: dimensional accuracy verified.*
4. **Assembly, Finishing & Customization** — primary assembly (overlock, cover stitch, flatlock), reinforced construction, seam finishing, sublimation printing, precision embroidery. *Checkpoint: assembly quality verified.*
5. **Quality Assurance, Packaging & Delivery** — in-line inspection, AQL final inspection, rework, labeling and traceability documentation, continuous improvement via feedback collection. *Checkpoint: batch accepted, shipment authorized.*
